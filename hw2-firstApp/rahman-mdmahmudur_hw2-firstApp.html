About Me

I am a Phd student at Cognitive Ubiquitous Computing and System (CUBICS) lab of Umass Lowell. My research focuses on designing and development of high fidelity mobile robot sensing technologies. I also have interest on working with medical imaging and health care data. My research interest lies in the intersect of Robotics, computer vision, signal processing and deep learning. I have recieved my B.S in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology in 2017. I worked in Hiperdyne Corporation, Tokyo, Japan from 2018 to 2019 as Machine Learning Engineer. I love to travel, hiking, mountain biking, skiing and other outdoor activities in my leisure time.
